Intro

Rodent issues persist as a critical concern in NYC, impacting public health, infrastructure, and daily life. The city's dense population and bustling environment provide rodents with abundant food and shelter, fueling their rapid increase. To tackle this urban challenge, the New York City government appointed its inaugural Rat Czar in 2023, signaling a focused approach to address this issue.

Utilizing data from the NYC Open Data Portal's 311 Service Requests dataset and the United States Census Bureau's American Community Survey for 2020, this visualization tool allows users to explore variations in rodent reports based on location, time of day, case status, season, borough, population density, and household income. This analysis offers insights into the nuanced nature of rodent occurrences across the cityscape.

Summary

At the end of 2020, over 70% of Rodent Cases Remained Open.

In summary, this interactive platform offers a comprehensive understanding of NYC's rodent issues by visualizing diverse data facets. By unraveling the complexities of rodent sightings across boroughs, demographics, and seasons, it equips stakeholders with valuable insights to craft targeted solutions. With a user-friendly interface and multifaceted visualizations, this tool empowers informed decision-making towards fostering a healthier, more resilient urban landscape for all New Yorkers.
